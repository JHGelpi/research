# Research Brief Template

This template defines the required structure for every research brief produced
by the research agent. Sections must appear in the order listed below. Do not
add, remove, reorder, or rename sections. Do not wrap the output in a code block.

---

## [Topic]

*(Replace with a concise title derived from the research topic — 5–8 words max.)*

---

## TL;DR

*(2–3 sentences maximum. State the single most important finding, why it matters,
and the clearest practical implication. Written for yourself — direct, no hedging.)*

---

## Background / context

*(2–4 sentences. What is this topic, why does it matter right now, and what
prompted the question. Establish the frame before diving into findings.
Cite any foundational facts: [Source Name, YYYY-MM].)*

---

Use [UNVERIFIED — needs source] for any claim that cannot be cited.)*

## So what / implications

*(Always present. 3–6 bullets connecting the findings to practical impact.
Answer the question: "Given what I just learned, what should I think, do,
or investigate next?" These are logical inferences from the evidence — not
opinions. Each implication should map to at least one finding above.)*

- Implication one
- Implication two
- Implication three

---

## Counterpoints / caveats

*(2–4 bullets. What does this research NOT show? What are the limitations of
the sources, the sample sizes, the recency? What reasonable counterargument
exists? This section protects against overconfidence when writing from this brief.)*

- Caveat one
- Caveat two

---

## Confidence flags

*(Table format. One row per finding or claim that carries meaningful uncertainty.
Only include items rated MEDIUM or LOW — omit HIGH-confidence items to keep
the table scannable.)*

| Claim | Rating | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Claim text | MEDIUM | Secondary source / older than 12 months / single study |
| Claim text | LOW | Inferred / no direct citation / conflicting sources |

Rating definitions:
- HIGH — primary source, published within 12 months. Not listed in this table.
- MEDIUM — secondary source, OR primary but older than 12 months [OLDER SOURCE].
- LOW — inferred, no direct citation, or conflicting sources exist.

---

## Sources

*(Numbered list. Every source cited inline above must appear here with its full
URL. No inline citation may be orphaned — if it appears above, it appears here.
If a URL is not available, note it as [URL unavailable].)*

2. Source Name — https://full-url-here (YYYY-MM)
